Hibiscus Darjeeling

January 5, 2018

Hibiscus flowers bring a rich red colour and tangy taste to the already vibrantly tart taste of Kombucha. We’ll look at making a 1 gallon batch of Hibiscus Darjeeling Kombucha.

  • 1 gallon
  • 7 days
  • 4-7 days

Ingredients

  • 16g of Organic Darjeeling Tea
  • 8g of Dried Hibiscus Flowers
  • 1 cup of Organic Cane Sugar
  • 1 Gallon of Filtered Water
  • 1 cup of Kombucha starter

Directions

  1. Boil 4 cups of water until a rolling boil
  2. Let water cool for 4mins
  3. Place 16g of Darjeeling tea into pot
  4. Steep for 15 mins
  5. Add Hibiscus Flowers after 6mins has elapsed
  6. Add 1 cup of organic sugar to 4 cups of filtered water
  7. Heat sugar water until fully dissolved
  8. Put sugar water into fermentation vessel
  9. Strain tea through a strainer (cheese cloth) into fermentation vessel
  10. Add remaining cold filtered water until 1 gallon is met
  11. Add 1 cup of Kombucha starter to fermentation vessel
  12. Ensure water temperature is between 60f - 80f
  13. Add Scoby and gently push down and mix in Scoby
  14. Ferment for 7 days at 78f

Secondary Fermentation

  1. Remove Scoby and place in a Scoby hotel
  2. Allow to ferment for 4-7 at days 70f in bottles before placing in fridge
